About Us

The Caribbean Graduate School of Theology was established in Kingston, Jamaica in 1986 under the sponsorship of the Caribbean Evangelical Theological Association (CETA), a consortium of some 50 under-graduate theological institutions scattered throughout the Caribbean region and belonging to various chruch groups.

Accredited by the University Council of Jamaica (UCJ), CGST operates interdenominationally and stresses commitment to the Christian worldview, academic exellence, contextual relevance, spiritual sensitivity and practical involvement. The school's purpose is to participate in shaping the future of the Caribbean by training men and women who have a passion to change our region for the better by the application of biblical principles to professional life.

In her 31-year history, the Graduate school has trained several hundred graduates who are serving across the length and breadth of the Caribbean. Her ministry also reaches beyond the Caribbean region to such places as the United States, Canada, the United Kingdom and Africa.

Many of our graduates occupy key leadership positions in the church. Others carry out their work in the secular domain, working in the media, the civil service, the school system, the correctional services and the business sector. Still, others are responding to the crises facing the youth and families in our society through the counselling ministry in schools and numerous agencies.

CGST is also a member of and, accredited by the Caribbean Evangelical Theological Asssociation (CETA), the regional accrediting agency for evangelical theological education, and a charter member of the International Council of Evangelical Theological Education(ICETE) of the World Evangelical Fellowship. In addition, CGST maintains her membership with the Association of Caribbean Tertiary Institutions (ACTI), a regional, professional body of tertiary leaders.
